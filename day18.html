Words that reach you when I cannot be right there

My Sabrina, my Jiji,

I keep laughing at myself because when I first started this thirty-day plan, I thought I would run out of ideas quickly. And in some ways I have. I already made playlists, cards, little games, memories, so now it feels like all that is left are these letters. But maybe that is not a bad thing. Maybe this was always supposed to be about the letters. Because in the end, Jiji, words are what I have. They are the way I can still reach you when I cannot be right there.

The truth is, I suffer a lot inside my own head. Having BPD makes me fight with thoughts that never leave me alone. My emotions burn too bright, every fear feels louder, and sometimes I cannot tell what is real and what is just my mind twisting itself against me. I get scared of losing you in ways that hurt me more than I can explain. I overthink your silences, I blame myself too quickly, and I carry guilt heavier than anyone ever asked me to carry.

There are days when my thoughts turn cruel against me, telling me I am too much, that I will push you away, that my love is too overwhelming. And yet, even in those moments, the one thing I cannot let go of is you. Even when my mind is at war with itself, the only thing that feels steady is my love for you.

I miss you more than I know how to explain. The night feels heavy without your voice. I find myself wishing you could see this sky with me, wishing I could point out the brightest star and say, that one is you. I picture you lying down somewhere, maybe tired, maybe lost in your own thoughts, and I hope you know that at the same moment I am here thinking of you.

Sometimes you call yourself weak, or a burden, or a doormat, but I need you to hear me clearly. To me, you are none of those things. You are my Jiji, the girl who keeps fighting even with the weight of the world on her shoulders. The girl who has walked through storms I could never imagine and still somehow keeps her heart soft. That is not weakness, it is the rarest strength I have ever seen.

And maybe that is why I keep writing these letters. They are my way of holding on when my mind feels like it is slipping. My way of proving to myself that even if my thoughts betray me, my love for you never does. Every word is a piece of me trying to quiet the storm inside, and to tell you again and again that you are my reason to keep standing.

Always yours,
Volon
